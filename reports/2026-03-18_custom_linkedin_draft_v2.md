By 2030, 45% of organizations are expected to orchestrate AI agents at scale, fundamentally changing decision-making processes across industries (IDC).

Agentic AI refers to autonomous systems that can operate independently to achieve specific goals, much like a self-driving car navigating through traffic. Just as a car uses sensors and data to make real-time decisions, agentic AI systems leverage advanced technologies like machine learning and natural language processing to adapt and respond to their environments.

Key findings from this research:

- Agentic AI systems can operate independently to achieve specific objectives, utilizing advanced decision-making frameworks (IBM, [What is Agentic AI?](https://www.ibm.com/think/topics/agentic-ai)).
- They possess the ability to understand and react to real-time data, allowing for dynamic interaction and workflow management (LeanIX, [Five key characteristics of AI agents](https://www.leanix.net/en/blog/ai-agents-and-enterprise-architecture-five-key-characteristics)).
- Major companies like OpenAI, Google DeepMind, and Microsoft are actively involved in creating models and standards for agentic AI systems (Designveloper, [20 Leading Agentic AI Companies & Platforms in 2026](https://www.designveloper.com/blog/top-agentic-ai-companies/)).
- Applications in healthcare include patient triage systems that enhance decision-making and operational efficiency (Infor, [Agentic AI Use Cases | 15 Examples in Industries](https://www.infor.com/platform/enterprise-ai/agentic-ai-use-cases)).
- Ensuring transparency in decision-making processes and mitigating algorithmic bias are critical for maintaining public trust (ProcessMaker, [Ethical Considerations of Agentic AI](https://www.processmaker.com/blog/ethical-considerations-of-agentic-ai/)).
